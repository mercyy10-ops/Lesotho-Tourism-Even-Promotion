Target Tourists and Locals

Thaba-Bosiu Cultural Village is a heritage and tourism site located at the foot of Thaba Bosiu, about 24 km from Maseru. It serves as a living museum that preserves and showcases the history, culture, and traditions of the Basotho people, closely linked to Moshoeshoe I, who used the mountain as a stronghold in the early 19th century to protect his people and establish the Basotho nation. The village features reconstructed traditional homesteads, guided tours, and cultural experiences that teach visitors about Basotho social structure, customs, and daily life. It also offers accommodation in traditional-style huts with modern amenities, making it both an educational and immersive destination that highlights Lesotho’s origins, identity, and cultural heritage.

This event is a celebration of Basotho culture, bringing people together to embrace our traditions, heritage, and identity with pride. It highlights the richness of our music, from traditional rhythms to modern sounds that define who we are today. Through this experience, we aim to create a strong sense of unity among Basotho while sharing our story with the world. The event also invites tourists to engage with and appreciate our culture in a meaningful and respectful way. By showcasing our unique customs, dress, and artistic expression, we make our presence known on a global stage. Ultimately, it is a powerful moment of cultural recognition, connection, and celebration.
